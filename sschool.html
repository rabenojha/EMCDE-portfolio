FOSS4G and SoTM

FOSS4G and SoTM 2022

I first came to know about the Free and Open Source for Geospatial (FOSS4G) conference when I was doing my undergrad back in Nepal. So, I have been following it for a few years now. As I moved to Europe last year to attend graduate school and FOSS4G 2022 and State of the Map 2022 were happening in Italy, I was really looking forward to both conferences.

State of the Map 2022

Date: August 19, 2022 - August 21, 2022

Venue: Florence, Italy


State of the Map is the yearly event that brings OpenStreetMap (OSM) enthusiasts from all over the world to one roof. This year in Florence, there were 407 people on site and 193 online attending the conference.

The 3 days were full of talks, workshops, posters, academic tracks related to OSM mapping, new tools and technologies developed around OSM, research and findings based on the data, works of the local communities in their regions, etc. And not to forget, the social interactions where I could meet many new people and catch up with the old ones.


Learn more about the State of the Map here.



FOSS4G 2022

Date: August 22, 2022 - August 28, 2022

Venue: Florence, Italy


Workshops

The first 2 days of the conference were full of workshops. As the workshops ran in parallel and were several hours long, I could only attend few.

Manage your geospatial data with PostgreSQL/PostGIS:

Learnt the basic introduction to SQL and PostgreSQL, the possibilities of using SQL instead of some Desktop GIS’s functionalities, storing and using geospatial data in databases, etc.The full material of the workshop can be accessed here.

Developing custom GRASS tools:

GRASS GIS, was a tool that I heard a lot about but never used before. In this workshop, the trainers showed us how to build on the Python scripts to GRASS tools, best practices in doing so, building Graphical User Interface (GUI) and Command Line Interface (CLI), and publishing it to the central repository. The full material of the workshop can be accessed here.


Main conference

The main conference started on 24th August with so many prominent speakers.There were presentations about the state of different open source projects, use cases and applications, challenges and opportunities in free and open source softwares, research works, etc.

Photo credit: Organizers


For those of you who are not aware of FOSS4G, it is the largest (to my knowledge) annual gathering of geospatial and related people organized by Open Source Geospatial Foundation (OSGeo). To give you the numbers, this year in Florence, 1303 people participated with 1044 on site participation. Learn more about FOSS4G here.

Acknowledgement: I would like to thank the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT) for funding my stay during the conference. And I am also thankful to FOSS4G organizers for giving the opportunity to volunteer at the conference.

HDCRS

IEEE GRSS HDCRS Summer School 2022

High Performance and Disruptive Computing in Remote Sensing (HDRCS)

Date: May 30, 2022 - June 02, 2022

Venue: Reykjavík, Iceland


Acceptance of Travel Grant

When I saw about the travel grant program to attend summer school, I applied for it. Luckily, after some days, I got an email confirmation from the organizers saying that I was one of the recipients of the travel grant.


Content and speakers

The summer school started with the opening remarks from Jón Atli Benediktsson, President and Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Iceland. During the first day, there were talks about ESA's Quantum Computing Initiative, an introduction to the Destination Earth project and a hands on session with Microsoft Planetary Computer. Not to forget, we also had a nice social dinner in the evening.

On the 2nd day, we learned about how the stencil computation is applied to preprocess hyperspectral images on shared memory systems. Álvaro Ordóñez Iglesias from the Universidade de Santiago de Compostela talked about registration of multi spectral and hyperspectral images on HPCs. There was also another interesting talk about CoE RAISE project and how they have been developing AI methods toward exascale computation.

Dr. Raúl Guerra from the Universidad de Las Palmas de Gran Canaria

The 3rd day included presentations from Dr Raúl Guerra about the use of parallel GPU models to enhance computationally high tasks in remote sensing and UAV applications integrated with low-power NVIDIA GPUs. In another session, Alison Lowndes from NVIDIA talked about how they have been improving the gaming sector with AI technology. All the sessions on the last day were about quantum computing. Prof. Piotr Gawron and Aleksandra Krawiec talked about the principles of quantum computing, different models of it, and how quantum computing is being used for earth observations.
